Insulating Aerial Lift Testing

Insulating aerial lifts are critical safety equipment for electrical utility workers. Our dielectric testing verifies that your equipment's insulating properties meet or exceed manufacturer specifications, ANSI A92.2 standards, and OSHA requirements.

Equipment We Test

  • Bucket trucks (boom-mounted and articulating)
  • Digger derricks and derrick trucks
  • Insulated aerial lifts and platforms
  • Material handling equipment with insulated booms

ANSI Boom Inspections

ANSI/SIA A92.2 standards require annual inspections of vehicle-mounted aerial devices. Our certified technicians perform thorough inspections to ensure your equipment meets all safety requirements.

Inspection Components

  • Hydraulic system testing

  • Load capacity verification

Testing Frequency & Compliance

Stay compliant with all industry regulations and safety standards

OSHA Requirements

OSHA 1910.269 requires dielectric testing of insulating aerial devices:

  • Before first use
  • Every 12 months thereafter
  • After any repairs or modifications
  • Whenever there's reason to suspect damage

ANSI Standards

ANSI/SIA A92.2 requires annual inspections of vehicle-mounted aerial devices to verify:

  • Structural integrity
  • Functional operation of all systems
  • Safety device effectiveness
  • Proper load capacity ratings

DOT Regulations

FMCSA regulations require annual inspections for commercial vehicles to ensure:

  • Roadworthiness and safety
  • Compliance with federal standards
  • Proper maintenance records
  • Driver and public safety
