How We Test Your PPE

Every piece of equipment undergoes rigorous multi-stage testing

1

Receiving & Documentation

Equipment is logged, inspected for obvious damage, and prepared for testing. Customer information and equipment details are recorded.

2

Visual Inspection

Detailed examination per ASTM F1236 for cuts, punctures, embedded objects, ozone cracking, UV damage, and any signs of deterioration.

3

Inflation Testing (Gloves)

Gloves are inflated to detect microscopic defects invisible to the naked eye. Any leaks or weak spots result in automatic failure.

4

Dielectric Testing

High-voltage testing verifies insulating properties. Equipment is submerged and subjected to proof test voltage for the specified duration.

5

Pass/Fail Determination

Results are evaluated against ASTM standards. Passing equipment receives certification; failed items are marked and documented.

6

Certification & Return

Passing items are date-stamped and labeled. Complete test records are provided with all returned equipment.

Regulatory Compliance

Stay Compliant with OSHA & ASTM Standards

OSHA 1910.137 and NFPA 70E require periodic electrical testing of rubber insulating equipment. Failure to maintain proper testing schedules can result in:

  • OSHA citations and fines
  • Worker injuries or fatalities
  • Increased liability and insurance costs
  • Work stoppages and lost productivity

We help you maintain compliance with complete documentation, accurate record-keeping, and timely reminder services.
