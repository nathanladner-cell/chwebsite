Our Calibration Process

Multi-point testing with NIST-traceable standards for complete accuracy assurance

1

Receiving & Inspection

Each tool is logged into our system with unique identification. Initial visual inspection documents condition and identifies any visible damage or wear.

2

As-Found Testing

We test your tool at multiple points across its range using our NIST-traceable torque standards. This documents the tool's accuracy before calibration.

3

Adjustment & Calibration

Tools are adjusted to meet manufacturer specifications or ISO 6789 standards (Â±3% or Â±4% depending on tool type). Digital tools receive electronic calibration.

4

As-Left Testing

After calibration, we re-test at the same points to verify accuracy. This ensures the tool meets required specifications across its entire range.

5

Certification & Documentation

Complete calibration certificates include as-found and as-left data, uncertainty calculations, traceability information, and pass/fail status.

6

Labeling & Return

Tools are marked with calibration date and due date labels. Complete documentation is provided digitally and in print, and tools are securely packaged for return.

NIST Traceability

Every calibration is traceable to the National Institute of Standards and Technology through an unbroken chain of comparisons:

  • Complete uncertainty analysis
