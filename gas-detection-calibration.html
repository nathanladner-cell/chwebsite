Our Calibration Process

Multi-gas calibration and bump testing with NIST-traceable standards

1

Receiving & Inspection

Each gas detector is logged into our system with unique identification. Visual inspection documents condition, sensor types, battery status, and calibration history.

2

Bump Test

We perform a bump test (functional test) to verify the detector responds to known concentrations of target gases and that alarms activate properly.

3

Zero Calibration

Detectors are zeroed in fresh air or zero gas to establish the baseline reading. This ensures accurate measurement of gas concentrations.

4

Span Gas Calibration

Known concentrations of NIST-traceable calibration gases are applied to each sensor. The detector is adjusted to read accurately at the span gas concentration.

5

Verification & Testing

After calibration, we verify accuracy with additional test gases and confirm alarm setpoints activate at proper concentrations. Battery and sensor condition are documented.

6

Certification & Return

Complete calibration certificates include bump test results, as-found/as-left data, gas types and concentrations used, sensor life remaining, and traceability documentation.
