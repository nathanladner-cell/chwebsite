Why Flow Calibration Matters

Flow calibration is necessary for industrial and commercial applications that rely on accurate flow rate measurements of fluids (liquid or gas). Accurate flow measurements ensure proper process control, regulatory compliance, custody transfer accuracy, and product quality. Examples of flow rate measurements are volumetric and mass flow rates. Various types of flow meters, such as thermal mass and laminar, measure flow rates for different purposes and require regular NIST-traceable calibration to maintain accuracy.

Our Calibration Process

Multi-point testing with NIST-traceable flow standards for complete accuracy assurance

1

Receiving & Inspection

Each flow device is logged into our system with unique identification. Visual inspection documents condition, fluid compatibility, and connection specifications.

2

As-Found Testing

We test your flow meter at multiple flow points across its full range using our NIST-traceable flow standards. This documents the device's accuracy before calibration.

3

Adjustment & Calibration

Flow devices are adjusted to meet manufacturer specifications or customer requirements. Calibration factors, zero offsets, and span adjustments are made as needed.

4

As-Left Testing

After calibration, we re-test at the same flow points to verify accuracy. This ensures the device meets required specifications across its entire flow range.

5

Certification & Documentation

Complete calibration certificates include as-found and as-left data, uncertainty calculations, traceability information, test conditions, and pass/fail status.

6

Labeling & Return

Devices are marked with calibration date and due date labels. Complete documentation is provided digitally and in print, and instruments are securely packaged for return.

Calibration Intervals

Recommended calibration frequency depends on usage, accuracy requirements, and criticality:

  • Custody Transfer: Quarterly to Semi-annually
  • Critical Process Control: Every 6 months
  • Regular Use: Annually
  • Light Use: Every 2 years
  • Reference Standards: Annually
  • After Contamination/Damage: Immediately
