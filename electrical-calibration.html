Why Electrical Calibration Matters

Electrical calibration refers to calibrating instruments that measure electrical parameters. Examples of these include: voltage, current, resistance, inductance, capacitance, time and frequency. Precise devices, such as a precise digital multimeter, can perform calibrations and verify the units under test (UUT). Accurate electrical measurements are critical for quality control, safety testing, regulatory compliance, and product development. Regular NIST-traceable calibration ensures your electrical test equipment delivers consistent, reliable measurements across all parameters.

Our Calibration Process

Multi-point testing with NIST-traceable electrical standards for complete accuracy assurance

1

Receiving & Inspection

Each electrical device is logged into our system with unique identification. Visual inspection documents condition, model, serial number, and any visible damage or wear.

2

As-Found Testing

We test your instrument at multiple points across its full range using our NIST-traceable electrical standards. This documents the device's accuracy before calibration.

3

Adjustment & Calibration

Devices are adjusted to meet manufacturer specifications or customer requirements. Calibration is performed using precision electrical calibrators and reference standards.

4

As-Left Testing

After calibration, we re-test at the same measurement points to verify accuracy. This ensures the device meets required specifications across its entire range.

5

Certification & Documentation

Complete calibration certificates include as-found and as-left data, uncertainty calculations, traceability information, test conditions, and pass/fail status.

6

Labeling & Return

Devices are marked with calibration date and due date labels. Complete documentation is provided digitally and in print, and instruments are securely packaged for return.
