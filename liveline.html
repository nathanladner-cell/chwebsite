Our Testing Process

Rigorous multi-stage testing ensures maximum worker protection

1

Receiving & Documentation

Each tool is logged with unique identification, photographed, and assigned a tracking number for complete traceability.

2

Visual Inspection

Thorough examination for cracks, chips, moisture contamination, physical damage, or any signs of deterioration per ASTM F711.

3

Cleaning & Preparation

Tools are cleaned to remove dirt, oils, and contaminants that could affect test results or insulating properties.

4

Electrical Testing

High-voltage dielectric testing applies proof test voltage for specified duration to verify insulation integrity.

5

Pass/Fail Analysis

Results are evaluated against ASTM F711 standards. Failing tools are marked and documented with failure reasons.

6

Certification & Return

Passing tools are labeled with test date and voltage class. Complete records and certificates are provided.

OSHA Requirements

OSHA 1910.137 requires electrical protective equipment to be inspected and electrically tested periodically.

  • Before first use
  • At intervals specified by ASTM standards
  • When visibly damaged
  • After any repairs or modifications

ASTM F711 Standard

Standard Specification for fiberglass-reinforced plastic (FRP) rod and tube used in live-line tools.

  • Proof test voltages defined by tool class
  • Visual inspection requirements
  • Test intervals based on usage
  • Pass/fail criteria established

Testing Intervals

Recommended Schedule to maintain compliance and worker safety.

Important Compliance Note

Using untested or failed live-line tools can result in serious injury or death. OSHA violations can lead to citations, fines, and increased liability. Maintain proper testing records and never use tools past their test due date.
